Making Room

Wednesday, July 15, 2026 2 minutes read

The first contribution to the new Seed Library wasn’t a seed.

It was a photograph.

A local gardener brought in a picture of their garden with a short note encouraging others to try xeriscaping. We pinned it to a small community board above the seed cabinet. It became the first story in what I hope will become a growing collection.

A few days earlier, I had been removing things.

The checkout log disappeared. We had never really used the information it collected. The brochure became a bookmark. I stopped worrying about maintaining a detailed seed inventory. One by one, I asked whether each part of the Seed Library existed to serve the library or to serve the person standing in front of it.

Some things stayed.

The bookmark stayed because someone might tuck it inside a gardening book and discover it again next spring. A featured gardening book remained because this is still a library. A small cork board stayed because it offered an invitation instead of another instruction.

Everything else quietly fell away.

I had been thinking about stewardship as preserving things.

Preserving the forms.

Preserving the procedures.

Preserving the pieces we’d always had.

But stewardship isn’t only about keeping.

Sometimes it’s about making room.

Making room for curiosity.

Making room for participation.

Making room for people to surprise you.

The first photograph convinced me that people don’t always want another place to record what they’ve taken.

Sometimes they’re looking for a place to leave behind what they’ve learned.

The old Seed Library asked patrons to document a transaction.

The new Seed Library invited them to contribute to a community.

Those sound similar, but they create entirely different experiences.

I don’t know how full that little cork board will become over the years.

I do know that the first thing pinned to it wasn’t a reminder of what left the library.

It was evidence of what came back.
