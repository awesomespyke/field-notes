Last year for Fabian's birthday, someone gave him one of those little suitcase record players.

At the time, neither of us knew any better. It played records, and that was enough.

One of the first albums to arrive was *Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 1*.

Around the same time, I found myself falling back in love with music in a different way. I spent months organizing my Plexamp library, briefly convinced CDs might be making a comeback, and even started burning discs again. Somewhere along the way I realized I was one of the few people I knew who still owned a CD player.

Vinyl kept calling.

So this year, for Fabian's birthday, we upgraded him to an Audio-Technica Sound Burger.

Naturally, *Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2* came home with it.

A few days later we couldn't leave *Crybaby* by Melanie Martinez behind. Then *Portals*. At some point I stopped thinking about whose records were whose.

Without really deciding to, we'd started a shared library.

Lately we've both been adding things that catch our attention, recommending albums back and forth, and discovering music together in a way that's become part of our friendship.

Right now the room has been filled with POP's *The Dream Is Over*, John Summit's *All the Time*, Ariana's *Hate That I Made You Love Me*, and The Chainsmokers' *Breathe*.

I don't think this is really a story about vinyl.

It's about the quiet joy of tending a library together.
